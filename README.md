# E-Commerce Sales Performance Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes e-commerce sales performance using Microsoft Excel to identify revenue trends, profitability patterns, category performance, and regional sales distribution. The dashboard enables interactive KPI tracking and supports data-driven business decision-making.

---

## Business Problem
The business required a centralized reporting dashboard to monitor sales performance, evaluate profitability,wanted to compare YOY trends and identify high-performing categories and regions.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze sales and profit trends
- Track key business KPIs like Toatl Sales,Profit Margin,No of orders etc
- Identify top-performing product categories
- Compare regional sales performance
- Generate actionable business insights

---

## Tools & Features Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- Slicers
- KPI Cards
- VLOOKUP
- INDEX-MATCH

---

## Dataset Information
- 1K+ transaction records
- Includes:
  - Sales
  - Profit
  - Quantity
  - Categories
  - Regions
  - Orders

---

## KPIs Tracked
- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Total Orders
- Profit Margin
- Quantity Sold

---

## Key Insights

### Revenue Analysis
- Sales increased by 20.62% YoY, indicating strong business growth.
- Q4 recorded the highest sales activity due to seasonal demand.

### Profitability Analysis
- Profit margin declined by 5.15% despite revenue growth.
- Revenue growth outpaced profit growth, indicating margin pressure.

### Category Performance
- Technology category contributed the highest revenue.
- Furniture category showed weaker profitability compared to sales volume.

### Regional Performance
- West region generated the highest sales volume.
- Certain regions showed weaker profit contribution despite strong sales.

---

## Business Recommendations
- Optimize discount strategies in low-margin categories.
- Increase inventory planning during peak seasonal periods.
- Focus marketing efforts on high-performing product categories.
- Improve pricing and operational efficiency in low-profit regions.
